Sleep Problems

Adjusting to a new environment and dealing with academic stress can contribute to sleep problems among international students.

Common Sleep Issues:

  • Insomnia: Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.
  • Jet Lag: Disrupted sleep patterns due to crossing multiple time zones.
  • Nightmares: Stress and anxiety can lead to distressing dreams.
  • Irregular Schedule: Irregular class schedules and study habits can affect sleep routines.

Tips for Better Sleep:

Consider these strategies to improve your sleep quality:

  • Establish a consistent sleep schedule.
  • Create a sleep-conducive environment: comfortable mattress, dark room, moderate temperature.
  • Avoid heavy meals and caffeine close to bedtime.
  • Practice relaxation techniques before sleep.
  • Limit screen time before bed.

Seeking Help:

If sleep problems persist, consider seeking help from a healthcare professional. Sleep is crucial for overall well-being and academic success.

Resources for Better Sleep:

Our campus offers resources to support your sleep health:

  • Sleep Workshops: Join workshops to learn effective sleep strategies and techniques.
  • Counseling Services: Connect with counselors who can help you manage stress and address sleep-related concerns.
  • Healthy Living Tips: Access more tips on maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including sleep.
